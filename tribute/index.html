Mahendra Singh Dhoni

Great Indian cricketer who played a leading role in the development of Indian cricket team

=>Mahendra Singh Dhoni is a former Indian International Cricketer who capitalized the Indian national Team in Limited-Overs formats from 2007 to 2017 and in Test cricket from 2008 to 2014,

Under his captaincy, =>India won the Inaugural 2007 ICC WORLD Twenty20, the 2010 and 2016 Asian Cups, the 2011 ICC cricket World cup and the 2013 ICC Champions Trophy.

=>A right-handed middle-order batsman and wicket-keeper,Dhoni is one of the highest run scorerer in One Day International(ODI)s with more than 10000 runs scored and is considered as effective "Finisher" in limited overs formats

=>he is widely regarded as one of the greatest wicket-keeper batsman and captains in the history of the game. He was also the First wicket-keeper
to effect 100 stumping in ODI cricket.

=>Dhoni had few batting opportunities in the first two games of the Sri Lankan bilateral ODI series (October–November 2005) and was promoted to No. 3 in the third ODI at Sawai Mansingh Stadium (Jaipur).
Sri Lanka had set India a target of 299 after a Kumar Sangakkara century and, in reply organisations. =>India lost Tendulkar early. Dhoni was promoted to accelerate the scoring and ended the game with an unbeaten 183 off 145 balls, winning the game for India.
The innings was described in Wisden Almanack (2006) as 'Uninhibited, yet anything but crude'.

=>The innings set various records including the highest individual score in ODI cricket in the second innings,
A record only broken after seven years by Shane Watson. =>Dhoni ended the series with the highest run aggregate and was awarded the Man of the Series award for his efforts.
In December 2005, Dhoni was rewarded a B-grade contract by the BCCI

=>2018: Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award. =>2009: Padma Shri, India's fourth-highest civilian award. =>2007–08: Major Dhyanchand Khel Ratna award. =>India's highest honor given for achievement in sports

=>Dhoni holds a honorary rank of Lieutenant Colonel in the Parachute Regiment of the Indian Territorial Army (106 Para TA battalion).
The honorary rank was presented to him by the Indian Army in 2011 for his service to the nation as a cricketer. =>After completing five parachute training jumps from Indian Army aircraft in the Agra training camp, he became a qualified paratrooper in 2015.
In August 2019 he completed a two-week stint with the Territorial Army in Jammu and Kashmir.

=>Under Dhoni's captaincy, India topped the Test cricket rankings for the first time, in 2009.
=>Dhoni has the most overseas Test defeats by an Indian captain, with 15.
=>Dhoni is the first Indian wicket-keeper to complete 4,000 Test runs.
=>Dhoni's maiden century against Pakistan in Faisalabad is fastest century scored by an Indian wicket-keeper.
=>After hitting a six in the third Test against England in Southampton, Dhoni completed 50 sixes as a captain.
=>Dhoni, with 294 dismissals in his career
=>Ranks first in the all-time dismissals list by Indian wicket-keepers.
=>Dhoni shares the record for most dismissals in an innings and in a match by an Indian wicket-keeper.
=>Dhoni is first Indian wicket-keeper to score 300 runs and take 15 dismissals in a series.
